Mental Health Support & Resources

Free and low-cost support options for students and communities

Free & Low-Cost Support

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline – Free, 24/7 support in the U.S.
  • NAMI HelpLine – National Alliance on Mental Illness free helpline.
  • Crisis Text Line – Text “HELLO” to 741741 for immediate support.
  • MentalHealth.gov – U.S. government mental health resources.

Student & Young Adult Resources

  • The Jed Foundation – Focused on young adult mental health.
  • Active Minds – Student-led mental health advocacy.

Helpful Tips for Teen Mental Health

  • Prioritize Self-Care: Get enough sleep, eat healthy, exercise, and take breaks from screens.
  • Connect with Others: Talk to trusted adults, spend time with friends and family, and join activities or clubs.
  • Manage Stress: Try deep breathing or meditation, enjoy hobbies, and break tasks into smaller steps.
  • Seek Professional Help if Needed: Reach out to a counselor, therapist, or call a hotline if you feel overwhelmed.
  • Encourage Healthy Habits: Set realistic goals, avoid risky behaviors, and limit screen time.
  • Foster Emotional Regulation: Express emotions in healthy ways, use coping skills, and practice gratitude.
  • Promote Mindfulness: Stay present, try yoga or journaling, and accept your feelings without judgment.
  • Cultivate a Positive Outlook: Challenge negative thoughts, be kind to yourself, and focus on growth.
